Kono On-boarding 4.0

2018


Background

TThe company’s participation in the TechCrunch hosted in Germany led to an on-boarding 4.0 project to increase subscribers. As a lead designer, I worked on the project with my team through a design sprint workshop.

What I did:

Proceeding with the design workshop
Lead Design(Research, UX/UI, Visual, Prototyping)


Design Workshop

First, the current on-boarding process was divided by elements. And we discussed the necessary elements of on-boarding with our team members and brainstormed each element’s ideas.

Key Factor
  • Drive user involvement
  • Tell user what a host/guest means
  • Show service benefits
Idea Sketch

During Google Design Sprint, I worked with my team to sketch ideas for each element through a crash 8’ skeetch.
We voted for ideas that would work best among the ideas, and we conducted design tests based on ideas that received a large number of votes.

Design Test

Based on an earlier activity, I created three design drafts: A, B and C. The design test shared the URL through the InVision.
With the help of TechCrunch officials, we received feedback on which of the three drafts had a significant impact on the Key Factor shown after the on-boarding.

With the C most-voteed proposal, I organized the entire on-boarding process again.

Outcome

The goal was to design, deploy, and re-modify quickly to meet the team’s development sprint schedule To unify the overall design style and focus more on where users enter values, I adjusted the font style so that the text can be seen better than the previous design.

I thought a lot about how to effectively express my team's ideas. In particular, from the host's perspective, I was concerned to communicate to the user that the guest receives a reminder mail and chooses a schedule, and that the guest/host is both scheduled to be created.
The precise animation effects were important as there were many elements on the screen, and for smooth communication with developers, I was able to talk about animation and collaborate efficiently by using the FramerX and Invision Studio.
